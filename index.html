Mission Statement

My mission is to make higher mathematics more approachable by publishing writing that emphasizes intuition, precise language, and intellectual curiosity. I want students to experience mathematics as a creative and humane discipline, not just a list of procedures.

About Me

I am an undergraduate at the University of Oregon studying pure mathematics. My work centers on clear mathematical exposition, especially for readers who are beginning to transition from computational mathematics into proof-based thinking.

I care deeply about writing that is rigorous without being intimidating. Through this portfolio, I share notes, essays, and guided introductions to topics in algebra, topology, and foundational mathematics.

Primary Occupations

Mathematical Writer: I produce expository articles that break down abstract concepts into clear conceptual steps while preserving formal correctness.

Student Researcher: My interests include algebra, topology, and category-theoretic methods. I enjoy exploring how structural ideas connect seemingly different parts of mathematics.

Educational Communicator: I design writing for students who want to rebuild confidence in mathematics through careful examples, transparent notation, and guided prerequisites.

Secondary Interests (Outside My Main Focus)

My central professional focus is mathematical writing. Alongside that, I maintain long-term interests in software development and music. These are secondary pursuits that inform how I communicate technical ideas.

Software: I enjoy graphics programming and compiler design, especially where abstract mathematical ideas become practical tools.

Music: I play jazz saxophone, which reinforces listening, pattern recognition, and disciplined improvisation that also benefits my writing process.
