About Me

Hello! My name is Paul Zupan. I am an undergraduate student at the University of Oregon studying pure mathematics and Jazz saxophone. Outside of school, I also have a passion for computers and programming.

Mathematics

My general mathematical interests are algebra and topology. More specifically, I enjoy studying algebraic geometry, algebraic topology, and particular applications of category theory. I am just the kind of person who is always thinking about some mathematical problem, and I love a good puzzle. My hope is to get a Ph.D. in mathematics so that I can teach in university and publish my work! I also have an interest in math pedagogy, and I love to write expository texts. That is the entire reason this website exists!

Explaining math is really fun for me, so part of my motivation to create this website was to post up some of my writing. I believe in destigmatizing mathematics and abolishing the generational trauma that seems to be associated with it. I think that even if you aren't good with numbers, there is still so much interesting math to be discovered. Math is not just about numbers. Rather, it is the study of abstraction and logical thought. If you want to know more about what I study or just want to learn about some cool math, check out the link to my blog below.

Software Development

Computer science and programming is a huge interest of mine as well, even though I do not study it in school. My original plan was to get a degree in computer science so I could work as a professional software developer, but I ended up switching to a math degree to avoid the CS job market hellscape. I am particularly fascinated with graphics programming and compiler design.

Graphics programming has been a consistent interest for me. It began as a simple desire to program video games, as many young people dream of doing. However, it was not the game design and art that gripped me, but rather the inner machinations of rendering to the screen. I have always found working with low-level APIs like OpenGL, Vulkan, and WGPU to be rewarding, since they teach you so much about how graphics cards actually work. From a software architecture perspective, I also enjoy the mental exercise of creating abstractions around the low-level APIs. Since I am a mathematician at heart, I also cannot help but find intriguing the math behind 3D rendering. I have always been blown away by how a 3-dimensional scene can be rendered to a 2-dimensional screen just using some simple linear algebra.

The other half of my programming interests is language programming. I find it very fun to pick things apart and see how they work, so it was only a matter of time until I did that with compilers and interpreters too. The pipeline of travelling from source code to tokens to abstract syntax tree to intermediate representation to assembly is infinitely amazing. As with graphics side of things, I just have to find a way to work math into everything, so parsing theory has also managed to capture my attention.

Music

While music is not my main interest, it is a big part of my life. I come from a musical family, so music was all around me throughout my upbringing. I have been playing saxophone since 2018, and I enjoy Jazz in particular. I play shows around Eugene with the UO big bands and combos. Come check us out if you happen to be in town!
